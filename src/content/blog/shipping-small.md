The projects I've finished all have one thing in common: they were
embarrassingly small when they launched.

The ones I abandoned? All ambitious.

## Scope is the enemy

A feature you can ship this week beats a system you can ship this quarter.
Every extra week of building is a week of zero feedback — and feedback is
the only thing that tells you whether you're building the right thing at all.

> If you're not slightly embarrassed by v1, you shipped too late.

## My rule of thumb

Before starting anything, I ask: **what's the version of this I could ship
in two days?** Then I build *that*. Not as a prototype — as the actual
release.

Most of the time, the two-day version turns out to be all anyone needed.
